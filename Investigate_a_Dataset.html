Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

  • 'PatientId' is a unique number of each patient
  • 'AppointmentID' is a unique number of each appointment
  • 'Gender' M for male, and F for female
  • ScheduledDay tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment.
  • AppointmentDay tells us on what day is the appointment.
  • 'Age' is age of each patient
  • Neighborhood indicates the location of the hospital
  • Scholarship indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Famlia.
  • 'Hipertension' indicated true of false of having high blood pressure
  • 'Diabetes' indicated true of false of having diabetes
  • 'Alcoholism' indicated true of false of having alcoholism
  • 'Handcap' indicated true of false of being disabled
  • 'SMS_received' indicated true of false of receiving a reminder SMS
  • 'No-show' with "yes" for not showing up and "No" if patient showed up

Conclusions¶

Several findings were observed from the brazilian medical appointments data set, such as:¶

  1. It was found that the gender of the patient has no effect on the likelihood of missing appointments.
  2. A relationship between the health status of patients and the likelihood of showing up for the appointment was found. Patients with health issues are more likely to no miss their appointments. This could be due to the urgency of the care in these situation in contrast to general appointment visits.
  3. SMS reminder effectivity was investigated,patients who received an SMS reminder were compared the ones who did not receive one. almost half of the people who received a message did not show up to the appointment, were that number was way lower at around 15% at the population who received the message. This might be due to the way the message mechanism worked, for example only send an SMS if the patient had already missed one appointment before. This information is not available, thus only speculations can be made.
  4. As time period of between the the appointment day and the day the appointment was scheduled, the more likely the appointment will be missed. This could be explained by the tendency of people to forget or get involved with other unforeseen activities which prevent them from meeting the appointment time.

Limitations¶

  1. Dataset might be biased to focus on no show side due to the scope and objective of collecting this data.
  2. More explanation of information can be beneficial on drawing more accurate conclusions.
